# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: March 2026*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** The sharp money tool for sports betting.

**What it does:** Delta helps bettors find profitable bets by tracking and following sharp money. We read exchange orderbooks and detect big bets, combined with bottom-up modeling for game markets and market movement analysis—showing you exactly where the sharp money is going.

**Product category:** Sports betting analytics / Sharp money tracking

**Product type:** SaaS

**Business model:** Subscription
- Weekly: $24.99
- Monthly: $79
- Annual: $299

**Stage:** Early traction (~$3.4k MRR, Month 3)

## Target Audience
**Target users:**
- Sharp bettors looking for edge confirmation and new angles
- Recreational bettors who want to become profitable

**Primary use case:** Finding profitable bets by identifying and following sharp money

**Jobs to be done:**
- Find where the sharp money is betting
- Stop losing long-term and start betting profitably
- Learn how professional bettors actually win
- Get an edge without building models from scratch

**Use cases:**
- Scanning exchange orderbooks for sharp positioning
- Detecting big bets and market moves before lines adjust
- Validating bet ideas against sharp consensus
- Learning profitable betting through exposure to sharp action

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Most bettors lose long-term and don't know how to actually be profitable.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- 99% of sports betting content and "education" doesn't help you win
- Most tipsters and picks services are noise
- Sharp money tools that work cost $100+/month
- Competitors treat sharp tracking as a side feature, not the core product

**What it costs them:** Consistent losses over time. Bankroll drain. Frustration from feeling like the game is rigged against them.

**Emotional tension:** Feeling like an outsider who doesn't have access to what the pros know. Wanting to win but not knowing what actually works.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** OddsJam, Upside Tools — treat sharp money tracking as a side tool, not the focus. Expensive ($100+/month).

**Secondary:** Action Network, BettingPros — more content/media focused, less actionable edge tools.

**Indirect:** Tipsters, picks services, betting Discord groups — promise wins but rarely deliver.

**How we differ:** Delta is built entirely around finding sharp money. Exchange orderbook reading is our core, not a feature buried in a dashboard. And we're significantly cheaper.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Find exactly where the sharp bets are
- Exchange orderbook reading as the primary method
- Bottom-up modeling combined with market movement
- More robust AND more affordable than competitors
- Brand/education angle that others lack

**How we do it differently:** We focus on reading exchanges and markets directly—where sharp money actually moves—rather than relying on line movement alone or aggregated "sharp" indicators.

**Why that's better:** You see the bets before the lines move. You're following real money, not lagging signals.

**Why customers choose us:** Cheaper than alternatives, more focused on what actually matters, and helps them learn—not just copy.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Losing money consistently. Realizing tipsters and picks don't work. Feeling like there's something they're missing.

**Pull:** Access to real sharp money data. Affordable compared to pro tools. Education component—actually learning how to win.

**Habit:** Comfortable with current losing approach. Already subscribed to other tools. Skeptical that anything works.

**Anxiety:** "What if I still lose?" "Is this just another scam?" "Will I actually understand it?"
